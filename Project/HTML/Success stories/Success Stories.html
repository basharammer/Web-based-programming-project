Success Stories

Super Mario

Super Mario Bros. is one of the most iconic and successful video games in history. Released by Nintendo for the NES, it revolutionized the platforming genre and helped save the video game industry after the 1983 crash. The game sold over 40 million copies worldwide and became a cultural phenomenon. Mario, the mascot of Nintendo, became one of the most recognizable characters in gaming. Super Mario Bros. laid the foundation for the entire Mario franchise, which remains one of the best-selling video game series ever.

The Legend of Zelda

Created by Shigeru Miyamoto and released by Nintendo for the NES, The Legend of Zelda introduced players to a vast, open world filled with puzzles and exploration. The game’s success is largely attributed to its innovative design, which blended action, adventure, and puzzle-solving. It became one of Nintendo’s flagship franchises, with Zelda titles like Ocarina of Time and Breath of the Wild later achieving massive critical acclaim. Over 125 million copies of Zelda games have been sold worldwide.

Pac-Man

Released by Namco, Pac-Man became a pop culture icon and a massive success. It’s one of the best-selling arcade games of all time, with millions of copies sold. Pac-Man was revolutionary because it appealed to both male and female audiences, which was rare for arcade games at the time. The game’s distinctive maze-chase gameplay, along with the character’s now-legendary yellow appearance, cemented Pac-Man as a symbol of the golden age of arcade gaming. It led to sequels, a television show, and various other media adaptations.

Galaga (1981)

Released by Namco as a sequel to Galaxian, Galaga became one of the most popular arcade games of its time. It introduced new features, such as the ability to rescue captured ships and engage in more dynamic, challenging battles against waves of alien ships. Galaga became a staple of the arcade scene in the early 1980s, selling millions of copies worldwide. Its fast-paced, strategic gameplay made it a favorite among gamers, and it has inspired numerous sequels and adaptations over the years. Galaga is considered one of the greatest shooters of its era and a cornerstone of arcade gaming.

Space Invaders

Released by Taito, Space Invaders was one of the first major arcade hits and played a key role in the early days of video gaming. It was one of the first games to create a sense of urgency and excitement with its increasing difficulty levels. Space Invaders led to the creation of an entire genre of shooting games and contributed to the golden age of arcade games. By the time it was discontinued, Space Invaders had grossed over $2 billion in revenue (adjusted for inflation), making it one of the most successful games of its time.

Centipede (1980)

Developed by Atari and released in 1980, Centipede quickly became one of the most popular arcade games of its time. The game combined elements of shooting and strategy, with players tasked with shooting a centipede as it winds its way down the screen, while also avoiding other enemies such as spiders and scorpions. Centipede was unique for its use of a trackball controller, which offered a more intuitive way to control movement. The game was especially popular among female players, which was unusual for the time. It went on to become one of the highest-grossing arcade games of its era, spawning numerous sequels and adaptations.
